Uruapan

Where can visitors enjoy the charms of a provincial city and the convenient features of an urban town?
Three reasons to visit Uruapan City.

Mexico showcases towns where modernization goes in hand with old time customs. Small towns that offer a unique contrast that can delight local visitors and travelers from abroad. Uruapan city, well known as The World’s Avocado and Guacamole Capital city, is a fine example of this blend. There are three reasons to consider visiting Uruapan city in your next vacations: unique gastronomy, outstanding markets to visit and great cultural-religious festivals.


Uruapan shares some of the gastronomy culture and international recognition that makes Morelia, capital city of the state, an international point of reference in terms of cuisine. An example of the intense and varied dishes served in Uruapan is Guacamole; Uruapan boasts the world’s 1st rank in avocado production, making this exotic fruit both available year-round and of the finest quality. The Uruapan guacamole experience is a unique and peerless one. Ice creams and ice bars turn to be another Uruapanse “don’t-miss-it” eatery. From sweet and creamy macadamia ice-creams to bitter-sour blackberry ice-bars, in this town you can find an exclusive variety of exotic ingredients. Entrenched competition among ice-cream producers makes it advantageous for visitors to engulf in this little pleasure. Finally, a great variety of tamales, corundas and uchepos can be found in Uruapan city. Corundas are normally not stuffed, pyramid shaped and salty small cakes. Uchepos are pretty soft and sweet. In contrast with traditional tamales which are made from corn flour, these are not stuffed and made of pure and fresh smashed corn.


In Uruapan City there are many markets where fresh and high quality local produce can be found all week. In La Charanda and San Francisco, the two best known, you can find the most exotic and the biggest variety of produce. Walking through the aisles of these markets gives a sense of living part of the real Mexican culture, barely known by visitors from abroad and highly experiential. If you prefer open spaces and green areas, Uruapan City is a great choice. The city has traditional places such as National Park, La Presa (The dam) and La Pinera, as well as newly created areas such as various new neighborhood parks known as Parques lineales.


Uruapan city organizes some of the greatest cultural, religious and artistic festivals in Mexico. Such as the pottery festival “Tianguis Artesanal Domingo de Ramos” (also known as Mercado de las Ollitas). This is the time and place where local artisans display their art, and visitors can find the anything from the cheapest and smallest piece of pottery, to the most elaborate and pricy ones. It’s an excellent occasion for making contact with original creative craftsmen. Another festival that has been growing year after year is Día de Muertos (Mexican holiday celebrating the death, when Mexicans show their devotion to passed away family members). In November 2 nd and 3 rd , Uruapan city organizes shrine competitions, tours around the graveyard and a respectable variety of artistic shows.


Uruapan is a charming small city that combines urban with provincial, great gastronomy with outstanding views, religious celebrations and cultural festivals. Uruapan city is catching the attention of more and more visitors. Festivals are specializing and small places like coffee shops, bars and restaurants will add up a unique experience if they invest in high end customer service.
